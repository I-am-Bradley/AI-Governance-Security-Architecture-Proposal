This document establishes a comprehensive framework for governing the design, development, deployment, and oversight of AI systems that handle proprietary, sensitive, or regulated data. It aligns with leading global standards and regulations, including the **NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF)**, the **EU AI Act**, and **ISO/IEC standards** (42001 for AI management systems, 27001 for information security), as well as privacy laws such as **GDPR, HIPAA, and CCPA**.

<br />The governance model defines clear **roles and responsibilities** across the organization from the AI Governance Board and Chief AI Officer to Data Protection Officers, Information Security Officers, AI Engineers, and Data Stewards. Each role is accountable for ensuring compliance, ethical use, fairness, transparency, and security throughout the AI lifecycle.

<br />Key policies include:
<br />**Data Governance & Classification** → Mandatory tagging of datasets (PHI, PII, internal, public) with audit logging and steward approval.

<br />**Accountability Framework (RACI)** → Explicit ownership and oversight for risk classification, bias mitigation, deployment approval, and incident response.

<br />**Human-in-the-Loop Oversight** → Required for high-risk AI systems to safeguard health, safety, and fundamental rights.

<br />**Policy Statements** → Commitments to responsible AI, fairness, transparency, privacy, and security, with enforceable controls and audits.

<br />**Risk Classification Framework** → Categorizes AI systems into low, medium, high-risk, or prohibited, determining approval workflows and monitoring obligations.

<br />**Lifecycle Management** → Defines end-to-end stages from ideation to retirement, embedding governance, compliance, and ethical review at every step.

<br />**Incident Management & Escalation** → Structured pathways for detecting, escalating, and mitigating AI-related incidents, with mandatory reporting for high-risk systems.

<br />**Monitoring & Recertification** → Continuous audits, drift analysis, fairness checks, and periodic recertification to ensure ongoing compliance and trustworthiness.

<br />Together, these elements form a **robust AI governance architecture** that integrates accountability, transparency, and human oversight into every stage of the AI lifecycle. The framework ensures that AI systems are not only technically sound but also ethically responsible, legally compliant, and aligned with organizational values.
