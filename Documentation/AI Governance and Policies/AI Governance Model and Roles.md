# 1. Governance Model
A robust AI governance model is essential for ensuring accountability, minimizing risks, and establishing clear ownership of AI-powered processes involving regulated or proprietary data.
<br />**NIST AI RMF – Governance:** "Governance functions help oversee and manage AI risks and ensure accountability across the AI lifecycle." (NIST AI RMF 1.0)
<br />**ISO/IEC 42001 – Organizational Roles:** "Top management shall ensure that responsibilities and authorities for relevant roles are assigned and communicated within the organization." (ISO/IEC 42001:2023)
<br />**EU AI Act – Provider Obligations:** "Providers of high-risk AI systems shall put in place a quality management system to ensure compliance with this Regulation." (EU AI Act, Article 17)

# 2. Roles and Responsibilities
## AI Governance Board (AIGB)
•	Provides strategic oversight for all AI initiatives
<br />•	Ensures compliance with global AI regulations and internal policies
<br />•	Approves high-risk AI systems and reviews risk mitigation plans
<br />•	Oversees ethical AI use, fairness, transparency, and privacy compliance
## Chief AI Officer (CAIO)
•	Leads AI strategy and ensures regulatory and ethical alignment
<br />•	Approves project proposals before Governance Board review
<br />•	Implements fairness, transparency, and privacy controls across models
## Data Protection Officer (DPO)
•	Ensures compliance with GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, and other privacy laws
<br />•	Approves Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs) and data minimization plans
<br />• Information Security Officer (ISO/CISO)
<br />•	Oversees security of AI data pipelines and infrastructure
<br />•	Ensures adherence to ISO 27001 controls
<br />•	Approves secure design reviews and threat modeling outcomes
## AI System Owners
•	Maintain end-to-end accountability for AI systems in their business unit
<br />•	Drive system documentation, performance monitoring, and compliance reporting
## AI Engineers / Model Developers
•	Implement models in accordance with responsible AI principles
<br />•	Conduct and document testing on bias, robustness, privacy, and security
<br />•	Maintain version control, reproducibility, and transparency
## Data Stewards
•	Enforce data governance rules for regulated and proprietary data
<br />•	Approve datasets before use in any AI lifecycle stage
## Audit & Compliance Team
•	Conduct independent review of AI systems, processes, and risks
<br />•	Validate compliance with internal policies and external regulatory obligations

# 3. Continuity and Integration
This governance model forms the backbone for the AI policies, risk classification, workflows, and approval processes described in the other documents. Each role is accountable for ensuring responsible, transparent, and compliant AI operations across the organization.
## Integration with Data Governance & Human Oversight
<br />To ensure enforceability of governance responsibilities and ethical principles, all AI system roles defined in this section shall operate in alignment with the **AI Data Governance, Accountability**, **and Human Oversight** controls. Accountability (via RACI), mandatory data classification, and human-in-the-loop decision-making for high-risk systems are integral components of governance and apply across the entire AI portfolio.
