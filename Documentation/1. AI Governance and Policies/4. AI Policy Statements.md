This document is part of the AI Governance Framework. It expands on the policies guiding responsible, fair, transparent, and privacy-compliant AI operations. These policy statements rely on the data protection, oversight controls, and accountability structure outlined in the **AI Data Governance, Accountability, and Human Oversight** section. Compliance with these elements — including data labeling, auditability requirements, and human validation — is mandatory for all AI systems regardless of deployment environment, risk level, or automation scope.

# 1. Responsible AI
**NIST AI RMF – Trustworthy AI:** "Trustworthy AI systems are valid and reliable, safe, secure and resilient, accountable and transparent, explainable and interpretable, privacy-enhanced, and fair with harmful bias managed." (NIST AI RMF 1.0)
<br />**Policy Statements:**
-	All AI systems must be designed and developed with ethical principles embedded
-	Bias mitigation strategies must be applied and documented
-	Model performance and reliability must be validated across use cases
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
# 2. Fairness and Non-Discrimination
**ISO/IEC 42001 – Ethical AI:** "The organization shall establish, implement, and maintain processes to identify and address ethical considerations related to AI systems." (ISO/IEC 42001:2023)
<br />**Policy Statements:**
-	All models must be tested for demographic, social, and operational bias
-	Processes for correction and mitigation must be defined
-	Regular audits to ensure fairness across system lifecycle
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
# 3. Transparency and Explainability
**EU AI Act – Transparency:** "AI systems intended to interact with natural persons shall be designed and developed in such a way that individuals are informed that they are interacting with an AI system." (EU AI Act, Article 52)
<br />**Policy Statements:**
-	Provide clear documentation of model purpose, capabilities, and limitations
-	Include model cards with explainable outputs
-	Ensure stakeholders can trace decision logic when applicable
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
# 4. Privacy and Data Protection
**GDPR – Data Protection by Design and by Default:** "The controller shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures for ensuring that, by default, only personal data which are necessary for each specific purpose of the processing are processed." (GDPR Article 25)
<br />**Policy Statements:**
-	Enforce data minimization and anonymization techniques
-	Limit access to sensitive and regulated data to authorized personnel only
-	Conduct Privacy Impact Assessments (PIA/DPIA) before deployment
-	Implement secure data retention and disposal policies
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
# 5. Security and Compliance
**ISO/IEC 27001:** "ISO/IEC 27001 is the world's best-known standard for information security management systems (ISMS). It defines requirements an ISMS must meet." (ISO/IEC 27001)
<br />**Policy Statements:**
-	All AI systems must comply with security policies, including threat modeling and secure architecture principles
-	Regular vulnerability and penetration testing
-	Document all compliance checks and audit results

# 6. Integration and Enforcement
These policies provide the foundation for risk classification, approval workflows, lifecycle management, and escalation procedures described in other documents of the AI Governance Framework. Each system and role is accountable for adhering to these policies to maintain responsible AI practices throughout its lifecycle.
