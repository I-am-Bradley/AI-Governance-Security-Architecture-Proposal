# 1. Purpose
To establish a clear and auditable pathway for escalating AI incidents, ensuring timely response, mitigation, and compliance with global regulations.
<br />**NIST AI RMF:** "Organizations should implement incident response procedures to identify, report, and mitigate AI system risks." (NIST AI RMF 1.0)
<br />**ISO/IEC 27001:** "Information security incidents shall be reported through appropriate management channels as quickly as possible." (ISO/IEC 27001)
<br />**EU AI Act – Serious Incidents:** "Providers shall report serious incidents or malfunctions of high-risk AI systems to the national competent authority." (Article 62)

# 2. Escalation Levels
## Level 1 – Operational Escalation
-	Triggered by minor anomalies, non-critical errors, or drift in low-risk AI systems
-	Managed by AI System Owner and engineering team
-	Logged in AI System Registry
## Level 2 – Management Escalation
-	Triggered by moderate risk events, medium-risk system issues, or compliance alerts
-	Reviewed by AI Governance Office, DPO, CISO, and CAIO
-	Immediate mitigation steps required
-	Reports to governance board if recurring
## Level 3 – Governance / Board Escalation
-	Triggered by serious incidents affecting high-risk AI systems, regulatory non-compliance, or potential harm
-	Full AI Governance Board engagement required
-	Immediate corrective actions and public reporting if mandated by law

# 3. Incident Types Covered
-	Bias or fairness deviation beyond defined thresholds
- Data breach or privacy violation
-	Security compromise or system intrusion
-	Operational failure causing harm to individuals or organization
-	Regulatory non-compliance

# 4. Process Overview
1. **Detection:** AI system monitors or staff identify potential incident
1. **nitial Logging:** Record incident details in AI System Registry
1. **3Assessment:** Determine risk level and classification according to risk framework
1. **Escalation:** Route incident to appropriate escalation level
1. **Mitigation & Response:** Implement fixes, controls, or rollback
1. **Reporting:** Communicate to internal and external stakeholders as required
1. **Post-Incident Review:** Update policies, workflows, and lessons learned

# 5. Integration with Monitoring & Recertification
Escalation and incident management are tightly coupled with ongoing monitoring. Quarterly audits, drift analysis, and annual recertification feed into incident detection and risk reassessment.
<br />**Integration Principle:** Escalated incidents may trigger temporary suspension of deployment, re-classification of system risk, or renewal of governance approval.
