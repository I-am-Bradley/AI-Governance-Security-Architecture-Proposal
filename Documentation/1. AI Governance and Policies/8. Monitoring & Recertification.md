This last section details ongoing monitoring, audit, and recertification procedures for AI systems. Short sections from Escalation and Global Framework references are merged to ensure document length and continuity.
________________________________________
# 1. Purpose
To ensure AI systems remain compliant, reliable, fair, and secure throughout their operational lifecycle.
<br />**NIST AI RMF – Monitoring:** "Ongoing monitoring is necessary to ensure that AI systems perform as intended and that risks are identified and managed." (NIST AI RMF 1.0)
<br />**ISO/IEC 42001 – Continuous Assessment:** "Organizations shall implement processes for ongoing assessment and review of AI system performance and compliance." (ISO/IEC 42001:2023)
<br />**EU AI Act – Post-Market Monitoring:** "Providers of high-risk AI systems shall establish a post-market monitoring system to collect, document and analyze relevant data on the operation of the system throughout its lifecycle." (Article 61)
________________________________________
# 2. Monitoring Activities
-	Continuous drift monitoring on model inputs and outputs.
-	Fairness and bias audits on a quarterly basis
-	Security and privacy audits, including access control verification
- Operational performance monitoring against pre-defined KPIs
-	Logging and tracking of incidents and near-misses
________________________________________
# 3. Reporting Requirements
**Low-risk systems:** Internal monitoring logs reviewed annually
<br />**Medium-risk systems:** Semi-annual audit reporting to CAIO + DPO + CISO
<br />**High-risk systems:** Mandatory 90-day reporting to AI Governance Board and regulatory authorities
________________________________________
# 4. Recertification Process
-	Annual review of AI system classification and performance
-	Update documentation, model cards, and risk assessments
-	Re-validation of controls, security, privacy, and fairness metrics
-	Governance Board approval for high-risk AI systems

Outputs include updated AI System Registry entries, renewed approvals, and audit records.
________________________________________
# 5. Integration with Escalation and Incident Management
Monitoring data informs incident detection and escalation
<br />Incidents may trigger reclassification, re-approval, or temporary suspension of AI systems
<br />Continuous improvement loop ensures lessons learned feed back into policies, workflows, and risk classification.
