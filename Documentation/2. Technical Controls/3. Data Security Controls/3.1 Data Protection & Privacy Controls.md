This is section  3.1 of the Technical Controls Section of the AI Governance & Policy Framework. It addresses security and privacy controls applied to regulated, sensitive, and proprietary data used across AI systems, ensuring compliance with global standards and privacy regulations.
________________________________________
# 1. Purpose
To implement robust data protection mechanisms ensuring confidentiality, privacy preservation, lawful processing, and responsible use of data within AI training, validation, and inference processes.
<br />**NIST AI RMF – Data Safeguards:** "Security and privacy safeguards are necessary to protect data used and generated by AI systems, especially when involving personal or sensitive information." (NIST AI RMF 1.0)
<br />**ISO/IEC 27001 – Data Confidentiality:** "Organizations shall ensure that information is protected from unauthorized access, disclosure, modification, and destruction." (ISO/IEC 27001:2022)
<br />**EU AI Act – Data Governance:** "Training, validation, and testing datasets used in high-risk AI systems shall be subject to appropriate data governance and management practices." (Article 10)
________________________________________
# 2. Privacy-by-Design Principles
•	Limit collection to **necessary data only** (data minimization).
<br />•	Embed privacy controls **at the architectural level.**
<br />•	Remove or transform personal identifiers when feasible.
<br />•	Maintain transparent lineage of how data is used for AI.
<br />Aligned with **GDPR Article 25 – Privacy by Design and by Default.**
________________________________________
# 3. Data Protection Controls
## 3.1 Encryption Standards
•	**AES-256** encryption for data at rest.
<br />•	**LS 1.3 or higher** for data in transit.
<br />•	Key management via **Hardware Security Modules (HSMs).**
<br />•	Mandatory key rotation and zero-trust integration.
## 3.2 Tokenization & Anonymization
•	Tokenize identifiers before model training.
<br />•	Mask sensitive fields used in inference.
<br />•	Apply k-anonymity or l-diversity where applicable.
<br />**ISO/IEC 20889:2018** referenced for privacy-enhancing techniques.
## 3.3 Differential Privacy
•	Add **noise** to sensitive records to prevent re-identification.
<br />•	Data Protection must be applied to any data leaving secure processing zones.
<br />•	Document privacy budgets (ε-values) for auditability.
<br />**NIST: DP ensures outputs reveal minimal information about individuals.**
## 3.4 Federated Learning (when applicable)
•	Data stays local; only model gradients are exchanged.
<br />•	Secure aggregation to prevent gradient leakage.
<br />•	Recommended for healthcare and financial data systems.
______________________________________
# 4. DLP (Data Loss Prevention)
•	Covers training datasets, embeddings, vector DBs, and model outputs.
<br />•	Monitors anomalous queries, large-volume extraction, and sensitive patterns.
<br />•	Logs continuously reviewed for auditing.
________________________________________
# 5. Privacy Preserving for AI Outputs
•	Remove sensitive data from responses.
<br />•	Apply safety filters to prevent inference leakage.
<br />•	Ensure embeddings **cannot be reverse engineered** to original data.
<br />•	Redaction rules for downstream system hand-offs.
<br />OWASP LLM Top 10: Output leakage = **critical** vulnerability.
________________________________________
# 6. Compliance Validation
•	Data protection assessments performed **pre-training.**
<br />•	Encryption configuration must be verified for each release.
<br />•	Annual privacy audits required for high-risk AI systems.
<br />•	Automatic remediation triggers to access anomalies.
<br />Failure to comply results in suspension, remediation mandates, escalation.
________________________________________
# 7. Continuous Improvement
•	Privacy-preserving evolution tracked via regulatory updates.
<br />•	Apply improvements informed by audits, incidents, and new techniques.
