AI systems introduce unique attack surfaces and operational risks that require specialized incident response planning. This section outlines processes, playbooks, and regulatory considerations to ensure effective detection, containment, and remediation of AI security events.

## 1. AI-Specific Incident Response Playbooks
Traditional IR procedures must be adapted for AI-specific scenarios:
-	**Model Compromise:** Steps to contain and isolate compromised models (e.g., unauthorized access, extraction, or tampering).
-	**Adversarial Attacks:** Detection, mitigation, and recovery processes for adversarial inputs or poisoning attempts.
-	**Data Leakage via AI Outputs:** Procedures to identify and remediate accidental disclosure of sensitive information through model responses.
-	**Misuse of LLM APIs or AI Services:** Steps for addressing API abuse, exfiltration attempts, or excessive query patterns.
-	**Integration with Existing IR:** Ensure AI-specific playbooks integrate seamlessly with enterprise-wide incident response procedures.

**Key Deliverables:**
-	Predefined workflows for each AI incident type.
-	Clear escalation paths for engineering, security, legal, and management teams.
-	Role assignments for AI security incident responders.

**Note:** You can use the AI Incident Response Playbook in the documentation as a template for your enterprise.

## 2. Data Breach Scenarios Involving Models
AI systems can expose sensitive information in unconventional ways. Incident response must cover:
-	**Training Data Exposure:** Respond to incidents where proprietary or regulated data used in model training is leaked.
-	**Inference-Time Data Leakage:** Address scenarios where model outputs inadvertently reveal confidential information.
-	**Model Theft or Extraction:** Contain and remediate cases where models are copied, reverse-engineered, or exfiltrated.
-	**Cross-System Impact:** Analyze potential impacts on downstream systems, APIs, or users.

**Mitigation Measures:**
-	Quarantine affected models or data sets immediately.
-	Conduct forensic analysis on model usage logs.
-	Revoke API keys and credentials as needed.
-	Retrain or patch models to remove compromised knowledge.

## 3. Regulatory Response Readiness
AI incidents often trigger regulatory obligations. Preparedness includes:
-	**Data Protection Compliance:** Align responses with GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, or other relevant regulations.
-	**Breach Notification Protocols:** Predefine timelines and communication channels for notifying regulators, customers, and stakeholders.
-	**Documentation & Reporting:** Maintain detailed incident logs, forensic evidence, and mitigation steps for compliance audits.
-	**Coordination with Legal & Compliance Teams:** Ensure that AI incidents are assessed for regulatory impact before external communication.

## 4. Detection & Monitoring Integration
Effective IR requires proactive detection mechanisms:
-	**Real-Time Alerts:** Monitor AI system logs for anomalous behavior, unexpected outputs, or suspicious API activity.
-	**Anomaly Detection for Models:** Track prediction distributions, confidence scores, and input/output patterns to flag potential compromise.
-	**Centralized Logging:** Aggregate AI system telemetry into SIEM or security monitoring platforms for correlation with other security events.

## 5. Post-Incident Procedures
-	**Root Cause Analysis:** Identify the origin of the AI security event (e.g., misconfiguration, data poisoning, API abuse).
-	**Remediation & Recovery:** Restore AI services using clean, verified models and datasets.
-	**Lessons Learned & Playbook Updates:** Update AI-specific IR playbooks based on incident findings to prevent recurrence.
-	**Stakeholder Communication:** Notify internal teams and external parties as required, maintaining transparency and regulatory compliance.

## 6. Simulation & Training
-	**Red-Team Exercises:** Periodically simulate AI-specific attacks (adversarial inputs, model theft, data leakage) to test IR readiness.
-	**Tabletop Exercises:** Walk through AI incident scenarios with cross-functional teams, including security, legal, compliance, and operations.
-	**Continuous Improvement:** Integrate lessons from exercises into AI system monitoring, security controls, and IR workflows.
