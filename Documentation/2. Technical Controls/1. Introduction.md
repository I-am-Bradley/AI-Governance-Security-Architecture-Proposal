The rapid adoption of Artificial Intelligence (AI), particularly systems handling **Proprietary, Sensitive, and Regulated Data (PII/PHI)**, introduces critical financial and regulatory liabilities. This comprehensive **AI Governance & Policy Framework** establishes the mandatory requirements and technical controls necessary to manage these risks and ensure trustworthy AI deployment across the enterprise.
<br />This framework is strategically aligned with global regulatory standards, including the **EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF**, and **ISO/IEC 42001**.
## Objective & Value Proposition
The primary objective is to transition the organization from reactive security to **Proactive, Risk-Based AI Governance**. By implementing the controls defined herein, we achieve:
<br /> **•	Liability Reduction:** Mandating controls to protect against high-severity attacks (e.g., data poisoning, model extraction), thus reducing our Annualized Loss Expectancy (**ALE**).
<br /> **•	Compliance Assurance:** Establishing documented, auditable proof of due diligence for high-risk systems, minimizing potential regulatory fines and operational disruption.
<br /> **•	Operational Resilience:** Defining secure architecture and incident response playbooks (Red, Blue, and Purple Team strategies) to ensure continuous, safe, and accountable innovation across all deployment models (Internal, Hybrid, and Cloud-Only).
<br />This document serves as the foundational mandate for all teams—from engineering and MLOps to legal and executive leadership—to secure our AI assets and maintain our license to innovate safely.
