This is section 1 of the Technical Controls Section of the AI Governance & Policy Framework. It establishes the foundational technical safeguards for designing and deploying secure AI systems that handle proprietary, sensitive, and regulated data.
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
# 1. Purpose
To define the architectural security controls required to ensure confidentiality, integrity, availability, and trustworthy behavior of AI systems across their lifecycle.
<br />**NIST AI RMF – Secure Design:** "Security must be considered at all stages of the AI lifecycle to ensure protection against attacks on AI components, data, and outputs." (NIST AI RMF 1.0)
<br />**EU AI Act – High-Risk Systems Security:** "High-risk AI systems shall be resilient against attempts to alter their use or performance by malicious third parties." (Article 15)
<br />**ISO/IEC 42001 – System Architecture Alignment:** "Architectural design shall support secure and reliable operation of AI systems and risk mitigation throughout their lifecycle." (ISO/IEC 42001:2023)

# 2. Security Architecture Principles
•	**Zero Trust Model** applied across AI workloads and data paths.
<br />•	**Defense-in-depth** layered security for model, data, and infrastructure.
<br />•	**Least privilege access** to AI model components and pipelines.
<br />•	**Secure-by-design** architecture with continuous threat modeling.
<br />•	**Data protection by design** aligned with privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR).
<br />These **principles** must be integrated into:
<br />•	Data pipelines
<br />•	Training and inference environments
<br />•	Model deployment infrastructure
<br />•	Cloud/on-premise hybrid environments

# 3. Key Technical Controls
## 3.1 Zero-Trust AI Infrastructure
•	Continuous authentication and verification for all AI components
<br />•	Dynamic segmentation of compute, storage, and model endpoints
<br />•	Microservice isolation for training and inference workloads
<br />**NIST SP 800-207 – Zero Trust Architecture:** Mandatory reference for implementation
## 3.2 Data Flow Security
•	Encryption of data **in-transit** (TLS 1.3+) and **at-rest** (AES-256+).
<br />•	Signed and integrity-checked model artifacts and datasets.
<br />•	Secure trusted data ingestion pipelines only.
<br />•	Monitoring for anomalous or unauthorized data access patterns.
## 3.3 Dependency and Supply Chain Protections
•	Software bills of materials (SBOMs) for AI components.
<br />•	Strict governance for open-source library intake.
<br />•	Continuous evaluation for supply-chain attacks (e.g., dependency poisoning).
<br />•	Hash-based validation for model weights and critical files.
<br />OWASP: "AI components are a growing supply chain attack vector."
## 3.4 Model Boundary Protection
•	Inference API firewalling and rate limitations.
<br />•	Runtime filtering and output validation.
<br />•	Hardening against extraction, inversion, poisoning, and evasion attacks.
<br />•	**No direct access** to embeddings/vector stores for end users.
<br />**OWASP LLM Top 10 — Model Theft Protection Required**

# 4. Architectural Diagram Requirements
Every high-risk AI system must include:
<br />1.	Data flow diagram
<br />2.	Model card with risk classifications
<br />3.	System security boundary map
<br />4.	Identity and access map
<br />5.	Trust and dependency relationships
<br />Governance Board approval is required **before** deployment.

# 6. Compliance Validation
Validation must occur:
<br />•	**Pre-deployment:** Architecture review + penetration testing.
<br />•	**Quarterly:** Drift detection and security regression testing.
<br />•	**Upon major version updates:** Recertification required.
<br />Failure to meet architectural requirements results in:
<br />•	Suspension of deployment
<br />•	Governance Board escalation
<br />•	Required remediation plan
