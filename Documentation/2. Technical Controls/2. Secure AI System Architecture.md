This is section 1 of the Technical Controls Section of the AI Governance & Policy Framework. It establishes the foundational technical safeguards for designing and deploying secure AI systems that handle proprietary, sensitive, and regulated data.
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
# 1. Purpose
To define the architectural security controls required to ensure confidentiality, integrity, availability, and trustworthy behavior of AI systems across their lifecycle.
<br />**NIST AI RMF – Secure Design:** "Security must be considered at all stages of the AI lifecycle to ensure protection against attacks on AI components, data, and outputs." (NIST AI RMF 1.0)
<br />**EU AI Act – High-Risk Systems Security:** "High-risk AI systems shall be resilient against attempts to alter their use or performance by malicious third parties." (Article 15)
<br />**ISO/IEC 42001 – System Architecture Alignment:** "Architectural design shall support secure and reliable operation of AI systems and risk mitigation throughout their lifecycle." (ISO/IEC 42001:2023)

# 2. Security Architecture Principles
•	**Zero Trust Model** applied across AI workloads and data paths.
-	**Defense-in-depth** layered security for model, data, and infrastructure.
-	**Least privilege access** to AI model components and pipelines.
-	**Secure-by-design** architecture with continuous threat modeling.
-	**Data protection by design** aligned with privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR).

These **principles** must be integrated into:
-	Data pipelines
-	Training and inference environments
-	Model deployment infrastructure
-	Cloud/on-premise hybrid environments

# 3. Key Technical Controls
## 3.1 Zero-Trust AI Infrastructure
-	Continuous authentication and verification for all AI components
-	Dynamic segmentation of compute, storage, and model endpoints
- Microservice isolation for training and inference workloads

**NIST SP 800-207 – Zero Trust Architecture:** Mandatory reference for implementation
## 3.2 Data Flow Security
-	Encryption of data **in-transit** (TLS 1.3+) and **at-rest** (AES-256+).
-	Signed and integrity-checked model artifacts and datasets.
-	Secure trusted data ingestion pipelines only.
-	Monitoring for anomalous or unauthorized data access patterns.
## 3.3 Dependency and Supply Chain Protections
-	Software bills of materials (SBOMs) for AI components.
-	Strict governance for open-source library intake.
-	Continuous evaluation for supply-chain attacks (e.g., dependency poisoning).
-	Hash-based validation for model weights and critical files.

OWASP: "AI components are a growing supply chain attack vector."
## 3.4 Model Boundary Protection
-	Inference API firewalling and rate limitations.
-	Runtime filtering and output validation.
-	Hardening against extraction, inversion, poisoning, and evasion attacks.
-	**No direct access** to embeddings/vector stores for end users.

**OWASP LLM Top 10 — Model Theft Protection Required**

# 4. Architectural Diagram Requirements
Every high-risk AI system must include:
1.	Data flow diagram
1.	Model card with risk classifications
1.	System security boundary map
1.	Identity and access map
1.	Trust and dependency relationships

Governance Board approval is required **before** deployment.

# 6. Compliance Validation
Validation must occur:
-	**Pre-deployment:** Architecture review + penetration testing.
-	**Quarterly:** Drift detection and security regression testing.
-	**Upon major version updates:** Recertification required.

Failure to meet architectural requirements results in:
-	Suspension of deployment
-	Governance Board escalation
-	Required remediation plan
