# 1. Purpose
This document defines requirements for periodic risk reassessment and recertification of AI systems deployed across the organization. The goal is to ensure that AI systems remain compliant, safe, fair, secure, auditable, and aligned with current legal and ethical standards throughout their operational lifecycle.

This policy supports compliance with:
-	**EU AI Act — Recertification of High-Risk AI (Articles 43 & 61)**
-	**NIST AI RMF — Govern & Manage Risks Over Time**
-	**ISO/IEC 42001 — Periodic Review Requirements**
-	**ISO/IEC 23894 — Reassessment of AI Risks**

# 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
-	All production AI systems handling **regulated, sensitive,** or **proprietary** data
-	Systems producing automated or semi-automated decisions impacting individuals or business operations

**High-risk systems** must undergo **formal governance recertification** annually.

# 3. Objectives
Periodic reviews shall:
| Objective	| Description |
| :-------- | :---------- |
| Maintain Compliance	| Ensure the system continues to meet regulatory requirements |
| Manage Risk	| Identify changes in safety, security, or fairness |
| Validate Performance	| Confirm ongoing accuracy, robustness, and integrity |
| Ensure Ethical Integrity	| Detect emerging bias or harmful outcomes |
| Provide Accountability	| Maintain traceability and documentation of all evaluations |
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
# 4. Types of Risk Reviews
| Review Type	| Frequency	| Applies To |
| :-------- | :---------- | :---------- |
| Operational Risk Review	| Quarterly	| High-risk systems |
| Compliance Risk Review	| Semi-annual	| Medium and high-risk systems |
| Full Governance Recertification	| Annual	| High-risk systems only |
| Trigger-Based Reviews	| On-demand	| All systems |

**Trigger-based reviews** are activated when:
-	Monitoring indicates **model drift, bias, or performance degradation**
-	Ethical or compliance issues arise
-	Regulators update requirements
-	Significant changes in data sources or population groups occur
-	Model retraining or architecture changes happen
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
# 5. Recertification Requirements
Annual recertification (high-risk systems) must include:
-	Updated documentation (model cards, PIAs/DPIAs, testing results)
-	Fairness, robustness, privacy, and cybersecurity re-evaluation
-	Verification of guardrails and explainability controls
-	Updated risk classification and governance approvals
-	Public impact or adverse event review

All recertification outputs must be recorded in the **AI System Registry.**

High-risk systems **cannot continue operating** without an active certification status.

# 6. Workflow & Governance Approval
Steps:
1.	System Owner submits **Risk Review Dossier**
2.	DPO and CISO review privacy & security posture
3.	CAIO conducts technical and ethical compliance check
4.	AI Governance Board approves or rejects recertification

Decision outcomes:
-	**Recertified**
-	**Conditional Approval** (requires improvements)
-	**Suspended Until Mitigation**
-	**Retired / Decommissioned**

Rejected systems must halt operation immediately.

# 7. Roles & Responsibilities
| Role	| Accountability |
| :-------- | :---------- |
| AI System Owner	| Initiates review, provides documentation |
| AI Engineering	| Conducts testing, remediation |
| Data Stewards	| Validate data governance compliance |
| DPO	| Privacy & fundamental rights compliance |
| CISO	| Security audit & threat model validation |
| AI Governance Board	| Final approval authority |

Cross-functional participation ensures unbiased reassessment.
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
# 8. Reporting & Traceability
Each review must include:
-	Audit log record
-	Risk decision memo
-	Updated model lineage tracking
-	Revision history of datasets and parameters

Traceability proves demonstrable compliance during **external audits.**
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
# 9. Enforcement
Non-compliance with periodic review procedures results in:
-	Removal of production access
-	Escalation to the Executive Risk Committee
-	Regulatory reporting if legally required

The organization enforces a **zero operational tolerance** for uncertified AI.
