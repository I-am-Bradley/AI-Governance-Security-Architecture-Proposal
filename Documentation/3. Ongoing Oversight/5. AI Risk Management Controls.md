# 1. Purpose
This section defines the mandatory controls required to identify, assess, mitigate, and continuously monitor risks introduced by AI systems. The objective is to ensure AI solutions operate in alignment with the organization’s security, ethical, privacy, and regulatory standards throughout their lifecycle.
# 2. Scope
Applies to all AI systems across the enterprise whether:
-	Developed internally or provided by third-party vendors
-	Deployed in production, pilot environments, or as PoC experiments
-	Embedded into larger systems or used as standalone services
-	Processing internal or external data

Risk management controls apply from **initial design** through **retirement** of a model.

# 3. AI Risk Domains Covered
AI risk must be evaluated and mitigated across the following core dimensions:
| Risk Domain	| Examples of Risk |
| :---------- | :--------------- |
| Security	| Model theft, data poisoning, prompt injection, adversarial attacks |
| Privacy	| Exposure of confidential or personal data, inference attacks |
| Safety	| Harmful or inaccurate responses that may mislead or cause dangerous outcomes |
| Ethical & Fairness	| Discrimination, biased outcomes, opacity in decision-making |
| Regulatory & Compliance	| Violations of laws including AI safety, data protection, financial or healthcare regulations |
| Operational Continuity	| Model degradation, downtime failures, dependency on external services |
| Reputation	| Offensive content, misleading outputs, brand damage |

# 4. Risk Control Requirements
## 1️⃣ Threat Modeling & Risk Assessment
Each AI system must undergo:
-	AI-specific **threat modeling**
-	**Risk classification** using the organizational risk framework (Section 1.5)
-	Mapping to applicable **regulations & internal policies**
-	Documentation of controls and residual risks

Risk assessments must be approved prior to deployment.
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
## 2️⃣ Security Controls
Mandatory protections include:
-	Adversarial robustness testing
-	Input validation and output filtering
-	Model access control (RBAC, MFA for privileged access)
-	Secure APIs and encryption for training and inference data
-	Hardening against:
    -	Prompt injection (LLMs)
    -	Model extraction attacks
    -	Training data poisoning

Security must be validated at each version release.
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
## 3️⃣ Privacy Controls
-	Only approved data sources may be used in model pipelines
-	Personal data requires:
    -	Data minimization
    -	Anonymization / pseudonymization
    -	Differential privacy for high-sensitivity use cases
-	Privacy risk assessments for high-impact models
-	No reproduction of protected data in generative outputs

Models must **fail secure** if privacy controls are breached.
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
## 4️⃣ Transparency & Explainability Controls
Based on model risk classification:
-	Document human-understandable logic boundaries
-	Provide user notifications when AI influences decisions
-	For high-risk automated decision systems:
    -	**Right to explanation** must be supported
    -	Logical reasoning or confidence scores must be made available
-	Maintain approved **Model Cards**
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
## 5️⃣ Fairness & Bias Controls
Bias mitigation aligned to intended user populations:
-	Pre-deployment disparate impact testing
-	Ongoing fairness monitoring and remediation triggers
-	Mandatory human-in-the-loop workflows where full automation is not permitted
-	Avoid harmful stereotyping in generative systems

Fairness results must be included in model certification.
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
## 6️⃣ Human Oversight Controls
Risk-proportionate human oversight is required:
| Model Risk Level	| Oversight |
| :---------- | :--------------- |
| Level 1–2	| Automated allowed with governance logging |
| Level 3	| Human review required on critical outputs |
| Level 4	| Human must approve decisions or have override authority |

Automated override must be disable-able at any time.
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
## 7️⃣ Operational & Resilience Controls
-	Drift detection and alerting
-	Fallback logic for outages (e.g., business rules when AI offline)
-	Vendor service dependency risk evaluation
-	SLA-driven incident response commitments
-	Mandatory retraining triggers (data change, performance drop, vulnerability discovery)

# 5. AI Risk Control Lifecycle
Controls must be embedded at the following checkpoints:
| Stage	| Risk Control Activities |
| :---------- | :--------------- |
| Design	| Initial risk assessment, regulatory mapping |
| Development	| Security + fairness architecture applied |
| Validation	| Adversarial testing, explainability checks |
| Deployment	| Governance sign-off and control verification |
| Operations	| Continuous monitoring, re-certification |
| Retirement	| Secure decommissioning and data disposal |
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
# 6. Incident Response & Escalation
Model-related incidents must be handled per the AI Incident Response Playbooks, including:
-	Model behaving outside approved parameters
-	Safety violations (harmful content)
-	Bias or discriminatory decisions detected
-	Privacy or security breach

Severe events require **immediate rollback** or safe-mode activation.
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
# 7. Documentation & Auditability
All risk control evidence must be stored in the **AI Governance Repository**, including:
-	Updated risk assessments
-	Compliance and security testing results
-	Model version changes and logs
-	Exception and waiver approvals

Failure to maintain documentation will result in removal from production.
________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
# 8. KPIs & Governance Metrics
Leadership will track:
-	Number of high-risk AI solutions in production
-	Incidents or near-misses attributable to AI
-	SLA compliance on risk remediation timelines
-	% of models operating with current risk certification

These metrics are reviewed **quarterly** by AI governance boards.
